GOVERNMENT STRUCTURE

The Three Spheres of Sovereign Rule

Our mock government operates under a constitutional system. Choose where to place your loyalty: the Crown, the Commons, or the Sovereign Courts.

THE EXECUTIVE MONARCHY

His Majesty’s Crown & Court

At the apex of our nation sits the Sovereign. The Crown handles the aesthetic and ceremonial majesty of the realm. While remaining politically neutral, the Monarch grants noble titles, signs legislation into Royal Assent, bestows Knighthoods, and commands the Privy Council.

Main Positions
Sovereign, Prince of Wales, Lord Chamberlain
Core Duties
Royal Assent, Honors Lists, Ceremonies
THE ELECTED HOUSE

The House of Commons

The epicenter of raw power. Run for office, form coalitions, write legislation, or govern as the Prime Minister. Commons hosts intense, moderated legislative debates where members of the government and loyal opposition clash over domestic and foreign policies.

Main Positions
Prime Minister, Speaker, Cabinet Minister, MP
Core Duties
Drafting Bills, Debates, Prime Minister's Questions
THE UPPER CHAMBER & JUSTICE

The Lords & Sovereign Judicature

Providing wisdom and legal review. The House of Lords refines and inspects bills sent from the Commons, acting as the ultimate brake on radical legislation. Meanwhile, the Sovereign Court hears constitutional disputes, trials, and rules on Server Rules or election fraud.

Main Positions
Lord Chancellor, Justice of the Supreme Court, Peer
Core Duties
Bill Amendment, Royal Trials, Electoral Disputes
